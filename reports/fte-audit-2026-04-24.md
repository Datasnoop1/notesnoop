## Implications

If operator sees wrong FTE values, the code is not the issue. Candidate upstream causes:

1. **Rubric semantics mismatch with Belfirst / other sources.** Rubric 9087 is *average FTE*. Belfirst may be quoting rubric 1003 / hours, 9086 headcount, or end-of-year FTE. Numbers will diverge.
2. **NBB filing data itself.** Some filers put personnel info in rubric 1001 (headcount) instead of 9087, leaving 9087 null → we show nothing or stale.
3. **Stale year.** UI may be showing FY(N-1) FTE while the operator is comparing against FY(N).
